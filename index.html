MISSING MIGRANT INCIDENTS

Introduction

In an increasingly interconnected world, the pursuit of better opportunities often leads individuals to undertake perilous journeys across borders. The dreams of a brighter future, however, often meet the unforgiving realities of the migration experience. The Missing Migrants Project, an initiative spearheaded by the International Organization for Migration (IOM) since 2014, endeavors to shed light on the tragic stories of those who embark on these treacherous paths and, in doing so, disappear from our collective consciousness.

Number of Missing Migrants

From 2014 onwards, the International Organization for Migration (IOM) has recorded 9428 incidents where migrants have either gone missing or experienced fatality. Of these incidents:

  • 2641 occurred in North America
  • 2329 occurred in Sub-Saharan Africa
  • 2038 occurred in Middle East & North Africa
  • 1113 occurred in South Asia
  • 818 occurred in Latin America & Caribbean
  • 476 occurred in Europe & Central Asia
  • 12 occurred in South America

Number of People Dead or Missing

In the wake of these tragic incidents, a total of 39,759 lives have been forever changed, with individuals either losing their lives or disappearing without a trace. Among these souls, 25% were women, 62.8% were men, and 12.2% were children. And whilst these numbers are high, it is crucial to acknowledge the limitations of data collection in this context. The figures presented likely represent an undercount, a sobering reminder of the complexities inherent in tracking those who vanish along migration routes. These numbers serve as a stark reminder of the human cost behind migration, urging us to reflect on the stories and lives behind the statistics.

Types of Incidents

The tapestry of incidents leading to a total of 9,248 cases encompasses heartbreaking tales of drowning in treacherous waters, accidents that claimed 411 lives, and healthcare denied to 691 individuals when they needed it most. Hunger, thirst, and the harsh elements have relentlessly snatched away 1,154 lives, while violence has cast its dark shadow over 781 others. Yet, within this mosaic of tragedy, 2,519 cases remain shrouded in mystery, their causes hidden from the records. These numbers cease to be mere statistics; instead, they serve as powerful reminders of the human toll exacted by the arduous journey of migration.

Where are the migrants going?

Across North America, Africa, Asia, and the vast oceans, migrant incidents paint a somber picture of the challenges faced by those seeking refuge, prosperity, or reunification with loved ones. North America, with 3,459 recorded incidents, stands as a coveted destination for migrants facing an arduous journey marked by border enforcement measures, treacherous terrains, and the menacing presence of human traffickers. In Africa, where 2,282 incidents have occurred, diverse migration routes traverse harsh deserts and unforgiving waters, exposing individuals to the risks of extreme conditions, violence, and a lack of essential resources. Asia, with 1,453 incidents, grapples with its own complex migration landscape, marked by overcrowded boats, remote routes, and disparities in wealth, while the ocean, accounting for 1,745 incidents, serves as a challenging pathway, where desperate migrants often confront accidents and drownings. These regions and the perilous journeys within them underscore the pressing need for comprehensive international cooperation and humanitarian efforts to ensure the safety and well-being of those in search of a better life.
